Every idea that ends up here starts the same way: with a business I think I understand and a price I think is wrong. Before I write anything, I try to state the thesis in a single sentence — what the market is missing, and why it will eventually notice. If I can't do that cleanly, I don't have a trade yet; I have a hunch.

## What each note tries to answer

Three questions, in order. What is this business actually worth on conservative assumptions? What is the market currently pricing in, and where does that diverge from my view? And what would have to go wrong for me to be the one who is mistaken? The last question matters most — a thesis without a clear way to be proven wrong is just a story.

These are working notes, not recommendations. They are the record of my own reasoning, published mostly to keep myself honest and partly because writing an argument down is the fastest way to find the hole in it. If a position changes, I would rather leave the original note standing and write a follow-up than quietly revise history.
